Narayan Gopal

The late Nepali Singer, Music Director and Actor, who was also known as “Emperor of Voice”.

TIMELINE

Here's a timeline of Narayan Gopal's life:

  • 1939 – Born on October 4,in Kathmandu,Nepal, Asha Gopal Guruacharya and Ram Devi Guruacharya.
  • 1954 – At the age of 15, Narayan Gopal was introduced to music and started learning to play the harmonium.
  • 1955 – At 16, he performed publicly for the first time, marking the beginning of his musical journey.
  • 1957 – Recorded his first song, beginning his professional music career.
  • 1960 – Became widely popular due to his unique voice and emotional depth, particularly in the Nepali movie industry.
  • 1961 – Worked with famous Nepali lyricists like Byakul Maila and Pradeep Kumar.
  • 1962 – Gained fame with the hit song "Samjhi Baschu", which became a classic in Nepali music.
  • 1963 – Began taking on the role of music director for various Nepali movies, significantly influencing the sound of the industry.
  • 1965 – Regularly performed on Nepali radio and television, establishing himself as a household name.
  • 1968 – Played a crucial role in shaping modern Nepali music, blending classical and contemporary styles.
  • 1970 – Collaborated with Lata Mangeshkar, performing duets for Nepali films, which increased his recognition.
  • 1972 – Released hits like "Euta Manchhe Ko Maya" and "Yo Mann Ta Mero Nepali," which became national anthems of Nepali love and patriotism.
  • 1973 – Received awards for his outstanding contribution to Nepali music, solidifying his place as a musical icon.
  • 1975 – Contributed as the music director for several successful Nepali films, influencing their success with his compositions.
  • 1978 – Released multiple albums, further enhancing his legacy in Nepali music.
  • 1981 – Took on the role of associate professor of music at Tribhuwan University, mentoring the next generation of Nepali musicians.
  • 1982 – Starred in Nepali dramas and plays, showing his versatility and engaging in acting alongside his musical career.
  • 1985 – Awarded for his lifelong contributions to Nepali music and culture, including honors from the Nepali government.
  • 1987 – Collaborated with a variety of instrumentalists and composers, bridging Nepali classical and Western sounds.
  • 1990 – Struggled with health issues, including a serious lung condition, which began to limit his public appearances.
  • 1990 – Passed away on January 5, 1990, at the age of 50, leaving behind an immense legacy in Nepali music and culture.

During the recording of a song for "Dakshina", Narayan Gopal was joking with the music director, who suggested ordering ice cream. Another musician declined, worried it would affect their voice, but Gopal eagerly agreed. After eating the ice cream, someone remarked, "Is he crazy? It will ruin his voice!" Despite this, Gopal recorded the song perfectly in one take. After listening to the playback, the director was amazed at how great the song sounded, showcasing Gopal's unmatched vocal talent, even after eating the ice cream.

– Tulsi Ghimire
